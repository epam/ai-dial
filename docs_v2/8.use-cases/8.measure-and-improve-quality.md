# Measure and improve AI quality

Changing a prompt or swapping a model feels like progress, but without measurement it is a guess. This page explains why AI applications need evaluation to improve reliably, how DIAL supports an eval-driven build loop, and where the limits of automated measurement lie. It is for teams deciding to take quality seriously and for the developers who would instrument it.

## Why this matters

AI output is not deterministic, so the usual signals of software quality do not apply. A change that makes one answer better can quietly make ten others worse, and "it looks good in the demo" is the most expensive sentence in an AI project. Teams that tune by intuition end up trading one failure for another without knowing it, because they never measured the whole.

Evaluation replaces intuition with evidence. Define what good looks like, score changes against it, and a prompt edit or model swap becomes a measured improvement or a measured regression rather than a hopeful one. This is what lets a RAG application or an agent get steadily better instead of merely different with each release.

## What you can do with DIAL

DIAL provides [RAG Eval](../3.building-with-dial/4.evaluations/1.rag-eval-toolkit.md), a provider-agnostic library for measuring how well a retrieval-augmented application answers. It scores retrieval with metrics such as recall, precision, and mean reciprocal rank, and it scores generation with metrics such as faithfulness and answer coverage, judged by an LLM. You run it offline during development, against ground-truth data, to tune retrieval and prompting before shipping.

The point is the loop, not a single score. Establish a baseline, make a change — a different retriever setting, a revised prompt, another model — and re-run the evaluation to see whether the change helped, hurt, or did nothing. Over many iterations the application converges on measurably better behavior. RAG Eval is independent of [DIAL RAG](../2.understand-dial/3.capabilities/2.rag-in-dial.md), so it applies to any RAG application, not only DIAL's.

## Why DIAL over the alternatives

Eyeballing outputs in a spreadsheet does not scale and does not catch regressions you did not think to look for. General-purpose evaluation frameworks exist, but DIAL's RAG Eval is built for the retrieval-and-generation shape that most enterprise AI takes, with the relevant metrics already defined. Its honest limit is the limit of all automated evaluation: a metric is a proxy for quality, not quality itself, and an LLM judge has its own biases. Evaluation narrows the space where human review is needed; it does not remove the need for judgment on what to measure.
