# Centralized cost and usage governance

AI spend is easy to start and hard to see. This page explains why centralized cost and usage governance becomes urgent as AI adoption spreads, how DIAL gives you limits, attribution, and visibility at the gateway, and what the trade-offs are. It is for the people who own the AI budget and for the platform team that would enforce the controls.

## Why this matters

Token-based pricing turns every prompt into a small, invisible cost. A single enthusiastic team, a runaway loop in an application, or a leaked key can multiply that into a budget surprise that nobody saw building. When each team holds its own provider account, there is no shared view: finance sees one large invoice with no breakdown, and there is no mechanism to stop overspend before it happens.

Governance answers three questions that an invoice cannot. Who is spending — which team, application, or key? How much can each of them spend before the platform says no? And what is actually happening right now, while there is still time to act? Without these, AI cost is a quantity you discover after the fact rather than a quantity you manage.

## What you can do with DIAL

Because every request passes through DIAL Core, Core is the natural place to meter and limit. [Usage limits and cost control](../2.understand-dial/4.security-and-governance/3.usage-limits-and-cost-control.md) lets you set quotas per key and per role, so no single user, application, or team can exceed its allocation, and a compromised key cannot drain the budget.

Attribution follows from the same chokepoint. Every request carries identity, so usage rolls up by user, role, and application rather than arriving as one undifferentiated total. [Analytics](../4.operating-dial/6.observability/5.analytics-realtime-setup.md) turns that stream into near-real-time visibility, and the admin [monitoring dashboards](../5.administering-dial/8.audit/2.monitoring-dashboards.md) present consumption to the people who own the budget.

## How you'd build it

- **Set the limits.** [Usage limits and cost control](../2.understand-dial/4.security-and-governance/3.usage-limits-and-cost-control.md) explains how quotas attach to keys and roles.
- **Apply them as an admin.** The admin view of [usage limits and cost control](../5.administering-dial/10.usage-limits-and-cost-control.md) is where day-to-day quotas are managed.
- **Harden production cost.** [Cost control](../4.operating-dial/7.production-readiness/6.cost-control.md) covers the operational practices that keep spend predictable at scale.
- **Get visibility.** [Analytics realtime setup](../4.operating-dial/6.observability/5.analytics-realtime-setup.md) and [monitoring and dashboards](../5.administering-dial/8.audit/2.monitoring-dashboards.md) turn raw usage into reports.

## Why DIAL over the alternatives

Provider-side spending caps exist, but they are per-vendor, coarse, and blind to which of your teams drove the cost. Spreadsheet reconciliation after the invoice arrives is attribution without control: it tells you what happened but cannot prevent it. DIAL governs at the gateway, before the request reaches the provider, so limits are enforced and attribution is captured at the moment of use. The cost is that all traffic must flow through Core, which is the same architecture that gives you access control and audit.

This governance pays off precisely when AI use is growing across many teams and models. For a single team on one provider, the provider's own dashboard may be enough until the second team arrives.
